A sore throat is usually mild and short-lived, often linked to dryness, irritation, or the early stages of a cold.

**When to see a doctor:** A sore throat lasting more than a week, severe pain, difficulty swallowing or breathing, or a high fever should be evaluated by a medical professional. This content is educational and not a substitute for professional medical advice.
