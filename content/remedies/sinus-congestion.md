Sinus congestion is usually linked to colds, allergies, or irritants, and often improves with simple home care.

**When to see a doctor:** Congestion lasting more than 10 days, high fever, facial swelling, or severe pain should be evaluated by a medical professional. This content is educational and not a substitute for professional medical advice.
